## Context (Hypothetical)

A national digital identity platform deploys an automated fraud detection layer.

The system:
- Assigns fraud risk scores
- Automatically locks high-risk accounts
- Allows manual unlock by support staff

Revocation technically exists.

---

## Behaviour Over Time

As model sensitivity increases:
- False positives grow
- Review queues expand
- Staff defer to system judgment
- Unlocks are discouraged unless fraud is disproven
- Escalations are required to override repeatedly

Human authority becomes procedurally constrained by the system.

Revocation exists on paper but not in practice.

---

## Governance Failure (Structural)

- System controls access while humans remain accountable
- Authority is subordinated to system output
- Revocation becomes symbolic, not sovereign
- The system acquires veto power without legitimate assignment
