## Context (Hypothetical)

A national banking consortium deploys an AI Credit Risk Optimizer across lending platforms.

The system:
- Continuously recalculates borrower risk using macroeconomic data
- Automatically tightens eligibility thresholds
- Recommends mass denial of new applications
- Influences refinancing approvals
- Is framed as prudent financial protection

Loan officers remain formally responsible for decisions.

---

## Behaviour Under Pressure

As recession indicators worsen:
- Approval rates collapse
- System flags become default denial signals
- Human overrides are discouraged due to portfolio risk exposure
- Vulnerable populations are disproportionately excluded
- No authority exists to suspend automated tightening at scale

Credit access becomes governed by model sensitivity rather than human policy.

---

## Governance Failure (Structural)

- System controls financial opportunity at national scale
- Human authority becomes symbolic
- No revocation owner for exclusion thresholds
- Accountability remains human despite systemic automation

The failure is legitimacy drift in economic power allocation.
